What is a parent function?
The most basic function in a family of functions. Example: f(x) = x is the parent of all linear functions, f(x) = x² is the parent of all quadratics.

⚽ Soccer — Sprint Speed Analysis

During a beep test, you cover 20 meters per shuttle. Your first shuttle takes 6 seconds, and each subsequent shuttle takes 0.5 seconds longer due to fatigue.

a) Write a linear function f(n) for the time of the nth shuttle.

b) How long will your 14th shuttle take?

c) If the beep test level requires completing a shuttle in under 10 seconds, what's the maximum shuttle number you can complete?

a) f(n) = 0.5n + 5.5 (slope = 0.5 sec/shuttle, first shuttle at n=1 gives 6)

b) f(14) = 0.5(14) + 5.5 = 12.5 seconds

c) Solve 0.5n + 5.5 < 10 → 0.5n < 4.5 → n < 9. Max shuttle = 8

This is a linear function with slope (rate of fatigue) and y-intercept (base time). The same concept as Section 1.1!

🏀 Basketball — Vertical Jump Training

You start PJF Vert Code with a standing vertical jump of 24 inches. The program claims you'll gain about 1.5 inches per week for the first 8 weeks.

a) Write a function V(w) for your vertical jump after w weeks.

b) A 10-foot rim is 120 inches. You're 5'10" (70 inches) with a standing reach of 88 inches. How many inches of vertical do you need to touch rim?

c) After how many weeks would the model predict you can touch rim?

a) V(w) = 1.5w + 24

b) Need: 120 - 88 = 32 inches of vertical to touch rim

c) Solve 1.5w + 24 ≥ 32 → 1.5w ≥ 8 → w ≥ 5.33 → After 6 weeks of training!

Linear model with slope = rate of improvement. Real talk: gains slow down after 8 weeks (non-linear), but this is a solid starting model.

⚽ Soccer — Pass Completion System

In a match, you attempt x short passes and y long passes. Short passes have a 90% completion rate, long passes have 60%. You want at least 20 completed passes total, and you attempt no more than 30 passes total.

a) Write a system of inequalities.

b) If you attempt 18 short passes and 12 long passes, do you meet both conditions?

c) Graph the system (or describe the feasible region).

a) System: { 0.9x + 0.6y ≥ 20 and x + y ≤ 30, x ≥ 0, y ≥ 0 }

b) Check: 0.9(18) + 0.6(12) = 16.2 + 7.2 = 23.4 ≥ 20 ✓ and 18 + 12 = 30 ≤ 30 ✓ — Yes!

c) The feasible region is the area where both shaded regions overlap, bounded by the two lines and the axes. All points in that region represent valid pass combinations.

This is a system of linear inequalities — Section 1.4 material. You'll see this on the test.

🏀 Basketball — Points Per Game Trend

Your scoring over 6 games: (1, 8), (2, 11), (3, 10), (4, 14), (5, 13), (6, 16).

a) Plot these points and describe the correlation.

b) Estimate the line of best fit (use two points that seem to represent the trend).

c) Predict your scoring in game 10 using your line.

a) Positive correlation — as games increase, points generally increase.

b) Using (1, 8) and (6, 16): slope = (16-8)/(6-1) = 8/5 = 1.6. Equation: y - 8 = 1.6(x - 1) → y = 1.6x + 6.4

c) y = 1.6(10) + 6.4 = 22.4 → About 22 points in game 10!

Scatter plots, correlation, and linear regression — Section 1.3 material. The same math scouts use to analyze player development.
